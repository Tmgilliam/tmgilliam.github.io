# Recognition strategy — intentional, not trophy-first

We do not optimize for “winning awards.” We optimize for **recruiter confidence, technical authority, and evidence**. Recognition is an outcome of that reputation—not the primary objective.

## Three classes of recognition (different ROI)

| Class | Purpose | Audience | Examples |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **1. Prestige awards** | High competition, major brand value | Broad industry / press | Stevie, Webby, CODiE |
| **2. Industry credibility** | What hiring managers and CTOs recognize | FAANG, Microsoft, AWS, enterprise | Public releases, architecture proof, governed AI methods |
| **3. Professional recognition** | Positions you as an expert over time | Peers, communities, nominators | Microsoft MVP, GitHub Stars, GDE, AWS Community Builder |

**Default priority order for daily work:** Class 2 → Class 3 → Class 1.

Prestige awards are entered only when a flagship already meets the Award Readiness Matrix (≥85) and published judging criteria.

## Primary niche (defend this)

> **Evidence-first enterprise AI** — governed systems where consequential actions are authorized, traceable, and supported by evidence.

BBI should become associated with that niche—not compete as a generic AI startup. Public Proof Gates, engineering governance, AI Readiness diagnostics, human-governed agent patterns, transparent roadmaps, and release discipline are the proof system for that reputation.

## What we will not chase

Avoid programs that:

- guarantee recognition after payment
- lack transparent judging criteria
- have no recognizable winners
- require purchasing trophies or publicity packages
- have little recognition among technical hiring managers

Paid entry fees are acceptable when they buy **independent evaluation**, not a badge.

## Operating rules

1. Criteria first, then build.
2. Matrix score **≥85** required to submit (no vanity entries).
3. Deadline pressure never invents metrics or exposes private systems.
4. First recognition priority is reputation for **evidence-first enterprise AI**.
5. Display results honestly on `/recognition`—never “award-winning” for Submitted / Preparing.
